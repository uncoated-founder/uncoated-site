What Uncoated Is

Scores are generated by applying a fixed, publicly documented formula to data sourced from government regulatory databases and open-data repositories. Nature of Product Scores

Product scores are opinions derived from applying a systematic methodology to publicly available data. They are provided as a consumer information tool, not as a definitive assessment of any product's safety, efficacy, or environmental impact.

  • Scores may not reflect the most current product formulation. Manufacturers may change ingredients or packaging at any time without notice.
  • A low score does not mean a product is unsafe, defective, or illegal. A high score does not guarantee a product is safe for you personally.
  • Risk classifications reflect what regulatory bodies have flagged based on available research. Science evolves, and so do our scores.
  • Grade labels (e.g. "Worth Switching", "Room to Improve") are shorthand for score ranges — they express our opinion about relative product quality, not factual claims about safety.
